Research on the Bronzo typeface

In a study project on typography, I explored the Bronzo typeface.I created a type specimen using HTML and CSS to showcase the unique characteristics and energy of Bronzo. Additionally, I designed a series of posters in Adobe Illustrator to further explore the expressive style of this typeface. This mix of digital and graphic design helped me gain a deeper understanding of Bronzo and present its potential effectively.

Visual language

In this course I made a parody on the legendary 1974, The Exorcist movie poster. I learned communication by exploring Semiotics. By Using Gestalt principles, I established visual hierarchy, balance, and flow, guiding the viewer’s eye. Finally, with Rhetoric, I persuaded through visuals, evoking emotion, building credibility, and communicating a clear message. By combining these elements, I created designs that are not only visually appealing but also impactful and memorable.

interactive interface

The course interface and interaction was all about educating the visitor in an interactive way. Me and a classmate made an interface in Figma to educate people on a healthy diet and how much excersice you need to burn calories. The calories of the selected products will be counted up. After you can tap the spacebar to make the runner move. Then the calories will be burnt after each movement. We translated real excerice into a digital implementation.

interactive digital exposition

A digital exposition exploring the rise of house music in the Netherlands. In this project, I created an interactive interface that brings together multiple media elements, including music, fragments of a documentary, and animated typography. The viewer can explore and interact with these elements, resulting in a layered and immersive experience that captures the raw energy of the early club scene and the atmosphere of that era.
